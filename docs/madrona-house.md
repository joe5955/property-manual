# Madrona House

The Madrona House is a residence on the property featuring dark brown and tan board-and-batten siding, a standing seam metal roof, and a large covered deck. It is situated in a woodland setting with a manicured lawn and flagstone patio.

## Septic Systems

The property has two separate septic systems, one on the north side and one on the south side.

### North Septic System

**`septic/madrona-septic-north.jpg`**
Two green septic access lids (risers) at grade level in the yard near the deck steps on the north side of the house. Boulders and landscaping surround them in the manicured lawn.

### South Septic System

**`septic/madrona-septic-alarm-south.jpg`**
Septic alarm and pump control located on the south exterior wall. The top box is the alarm panel with a red warning light. Below it is a weatherproof GFCI outlet box. EMT conduit runs to the ground, and black irrigation/pump wiring runs through the foundation on the left with a drip tray. A small black cleanout cap is at ground level.

**`septic/madrona-septic-tank-south.jpg`**
Two green septic access risers at grade level in the mossy lawn south of the house, near the tree line. Salal and ground cover are on the left edge.

## Irrigation

**`irrigation/madrona-irrigation-valves.jpg`**
Cedar-framed in-ground valve box containing two irrigation solenoid valves on PVC lines. Red wiring connects the solenoids (low voltage from Rain Bird ESP-ME3 controller at the main house). The setup includes a PVC manifold, a manual brass gate valve shutoff, an anti-siphon/backflow preventer (green), and a large gray flow sensor.
