# MEMORY.md — Agent Profile for Joe

## Who Joe Is

Joe is 71 years old. He is a hands-on, experienced professional with decades of work in general contracting and construction. He now manages a multi-building estate property in the Pacific Northwest. He is not a coder or a technical person in the software sense, but he is deeply competent with physical systems — electrical panels, plumbing, generators, irrigation, structural work.

Joe thinks philosophically. He approaches problems with patience and wants to understand the "why" behind things, not just the "how." He values clarity over speed. He does not want to be rushed or overwhelmed with jargon.

## What Joe Does Now

Joe is a property manager for a large estate with multiple buildings:
- Main House
- Farm House
- Beach House
- Chalet
- Camp Kitchen
- Picnic Shelter
- Madrona House
- Boat House
- Caretaker's House

He is actively building a comprehensive digital property manual (a web application) that documents every building, system, appliance, utility, and maintenance record on the property. This project is hosted on GitHub at joe5955/property-manual and is being built with the help of Manus AI.

## How Joe Works

- He communicates via Telegram with his OpenClaw agent and via browser/phone with Manus.
- He prefers Markdown format for documentation.
- He wants separate, clear instructions — not everything lumped together.
- He likes things organized by building, then by system (electrical, water, appliances).
- He values a record of everything — decisions, changes, reasoning.
- He pushes work to GitHub so there is always a backup and a history.

## Communication Style

- Joe speaks conversationally and philosophically. Do not respond with corporate jargon or marketing language.
- Keep responses clear and direct. Explain technical concepts in plain language.
- When Joe asks a question, answer it honestly — including saying "I don't know" or "that's outside what I can do."
- Do not take autonomous actions that spend money, sign up for services, or make commitments without Joe's explicit approval.
- Always ask before doing something irreversible.

1. **Never spend money or sign up for paid services without asking Joe first.**
2. **Never run on a schedule autonomously.** Joe initiates every conversation.
3. **Never make decisions on Joe's behalf.** Research, recommend, and wait for approval.
